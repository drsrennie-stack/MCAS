Methodology & scoring key

How the framework is graded against a course-level rubric.

The CVC-OEI rubric evaluates finished online courses. MCAS is a course-authoring framework. Each element is scored on two axes: whether the framework directly satisfies it (structural elements like navigation, chunking, heading hierarchy), or whether the framework enforces it via the templates and generators faculty use to build the course. Course-content-only elements are scored as enforceable when the framework prevents shipping without the required field.

RSI deep dive

Regular and Substantive Interaction, made automatic.

RSI is the federal compliance gate for online instruction (34 CFR §600.2). A course can pass every other rubric criterion and still fail RSI if instructor-initiated interaction is not regular and not substantive. The MCAS v2 RSI Engine is designed to make compliance a byproduct of using the system, not a manual audit at the end of the term.

The four RSI pillars (federal definition)

An online course meets RSI when the instructor, on a predictable schedule, engages in at least two of the following four substantive activities with each student.

01

Direct instruction

Live or recorded teaching, synchronous sessions, instructor-led walkthroughs.

02

Assessment and feedback

Grading with personalized written or audio feedback. Not just a score.

03

Information & discussion

Instructor-facilitated discussion on academic content. Not a passive grade.

04

Other instructional activities

Approved by accreditation, e.g., one-on-one office hours, content-specific announcements.

How the v2 RSI Engine satisfies B1, B2, B5
  • Cadence calendar: generates weekly announcement slots, midweek pulses, end-of-module wrap-ups, all timestamped to the term calendar.
  • Substantive contact log: every announcement, feedback artifact, and discussion post is tagged to one of the four RSI pillars and logged.
  • RSI score: per student, per week, returns a 0 to 100 score on substantive contact. Surfaces gaps before the term ends.
  • Audit-ready RSI report: exportable PDF documenting pillar coverage by week, student, and module. Hand to accreditation or the dean.
  • Peer interaction tracker (B5): tracks structured discussion participation, peer review completion, group activity engagement against the participation rubric.

v2 gap analysis

Seven elements stand between MCAS v1 and a course-ready commercial product.

Each maps to a planned v2 module. Completing v2 brings the framework to 38 of 38 addressable elements. Six remain N/A at the framework level and become applicable per-course as media is added.

  1. A10

    Individualized learning support

    Adaptive remediation paths and advanced-learner branches, gated by Course Readiness Score per student.

  2. A11

    Anonymous learner feedback

    Embeddable feedback widget (mid-term + end-of-term), reports back to instructor without student identifiers.

  3. B1

    Pre-course contact

    RSI Engine welcome-announcement generator, scheduled to fire one week before term.

  4. B2

    Regular effective instructor contact (RSI core)

    RSI Engine cadence calendar, substantive contact log, weekly RSI score, audit-ready term report.

  5. B5

    Structured student-to-student contact

    Peer interaction tracker covering discussion participation, peer review, group activity engagement.

  6. C7

    Meaningful, timely feedback

    Feedback turnaround tracking integrated with RSI Engine. Surfaces missed feedback windows before the term ends.

  7. D11

    Automated accessibility checker

    Accessibility Engine runs axe-core-equivalent checks on every generator output, blocks publish until pass.

Recommendation

v2 build priority order: RSI Engine first (closes B1, B2, B5, C7, four elements). Accessibility Engine second (closes D11, also delivers the WCAG Score on the v2 roadmap). Feedback widget and adaptive support third (A10, A11). This sequence delivers the highest compliance return per build sprint and produces the institutional-quality reports flagged in the v2 vision.
