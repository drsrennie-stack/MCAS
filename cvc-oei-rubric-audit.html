Methodology & scoring key

The CVC-OEI rubric is written to evaluate finished online courses. MCAS is a course-authoring framework, not a course. To audit fairly, each rubric element is scored along two axes: whether the framework directly satisfies it (structural elements like navigation, chunking, heading hierarchy), or whether the framework enforces it via the templates and generators faculty use to build the course. Course-content-only elements (e.g., the clarity of one specific learning objective) are scored as enforceable when the framework prevents shipping without the required field.

RSI deep dive

RSI (Regular and Substantive Interaction) is the federal compliance gate for online instruction (34 CFR §600.2). A course can pass every other rubric criterion and still fail RSI if instructor-initiated interaction is not regular and not substantive. The MCAS v2 RSI Engine is designed to make compliance a byproduct of using the system rather than a manual audit at the end of the term.

The four RSI pillars (federal definition)

An online course meets RSI when the instructor, on a predictable schedule, engages in at least two of the following four substantive activities with each student:

1. Direct instruction

Live or recorded teaching, synchronous sessions, instructor-led walkthroughs.

2. Assessment and feedback

Grading with personalized written or audio feedback. Not just a score.

3. Information / discussion

Instructor-facilitated discussion on academic content. Not a passive grade.

4. Other instructional activities

Approved by accreditation, e.g., one-on-one office hours, content-specific announcements.

How the v2 RSI Engine satisfies B1, B2, B5

  • Cadence calendar: generates weekly announcement slots, midweek pulses, end-of-module wrap-ups, all timestamped to the term calendar.
  • Substantive contact log: every announcement, feedback artifact, and discussion post is tagged to one of the four RSI pillars and logged.
  • RSI score: per student, per week, returns a 0-100 score on substantive contact. Surfaces gaps before the term ends.
  • Audit-ready RSI report: exportable PDF that documents pillar coverage by week, student, and module. Hand this to accreditation or your dean.
  • Peer interaction tracker (B5): tracks structured discussion participation, peer review completion, and group activity engagement against the participation rubric.

v2 gap analysis: seven elements to close

These seven elements are not yet addressed by MCAS v1. Each maps to a planned v2 module. Completing v2 brings the framework to 38 / 38 addressable elements (six remain N/A at the framework level and become applicable per-course as media is added).

  1. A10
    Individualized learning supportAdaptive remediation paths and advanced-learner branches, gated by Course Readiness Score per student.
  2. A11
    Anonymous learner feedbackEmbeddable feedback widget (mid-term + end-of-term), reports back to instructor without student identifiers.
  3. B1
    Pre-course contactRSI Engine welcome-announcement generator, scheduled to fire one week before term.
  4. B2
    Regular effective instructor contact (RSI core)RSI Engine cadence calendar, substantive contact log, weekly RSI score, audit-ready term report.
  5. B5
    Structured student-to-student contactPeer interaction tracker: discussion participation, peer review, group activity engagement.
  6. C7
    Meaningful, timely feedbackFeedback turnaround tracking integrated with RSI Engine. Surfaces missed feedback windows before the term ends.
  7. D11
    Automated accessibility checkerAccessibility Engine runs axe-core-equivalent checks on every generator output, blocks publish until pass.

Recommendation

v2 build priority order: RSI Engine first (closes B1, B2, B5, C7, four elements). Accessibility Engine second (closes D11, also delivers the WCAG Score deliverable on the v2 roadmap). Feedback widget and adaptive support third (A10, A11). This order delivers the highest compliance return per build sprint and produces the institutional-quality reports flagged in the v2 vision.
